    AI Acquisition Resource Dashboard

    The AI Acquisition Resource Dashboard is a tool for the acquisition workforce to identify government-wide contract vehicles that agencies have used to procure Generative AI and Specialized Computing Infrastructure.

    Public

    Sourced from FPDS

    Scoped to gen AI

Overview

The AI Acquisition Resource Dashboard is created to align with Executive Order 14110 Section 10.1(h). This order mandates General Services Administration (GSA) to facilitate agencies' access to commercial Generative AI and specialized computing infrastructure capabilities.

Data source

Updated monthly, the Dashboard draws from the Federal Procurement Data System (FPDS) to present data from FY20 onwards. The date of the latest dashboard update is prominently displayed next to the Dashboard title, ensuring users are aware of the currentness of the information provided. Our data review process encompasses all Level 1 categories, excluding Department of Defense (DOD) contracts and open market contracts, to focus on relevant governmental procurement activities relevant to the Executive Order.

To ensure the relevance and specificity of the data, we employ an AI-focused keyword search methodology. This approach utilizes a continually refined list of several hundred AI-related keywords. These keywords target the description of the requirements column within the FPDS entries, allowing us to filter and retain only those rows that are pertinent to Generative AI and Specialized Computing Infrastructure. Through this targeted search, we refine the vast dataset of over 30 millions rows to a manageable and highly relevant dataframe, typically encompassing between 3,000 and 5,000 purchase requests. This careful curation process results in a detailed snapshot of government purchasing behaviors related to Generative AI and Specialized Computing Infrastructure.

Views

Vendor count

The FPDS exclusively features Prime Vendors. The Vendor Count view presents the count of Prime Vendors according to the Contract Vehicle utilized. It distinguishes between solutions managed by individual agencies and those available government-wide. The purpose of this differentiation is to spotlight solutions outside the open market, focusing on Best in Class (BIC), Tier 2, and Tier 2 Small Business contracts, among other designations, especially for AI or specialized computing infrastructure solutions.

Level 3

Utilizing the tiered categorization system from the FPDS, this view offers a detailed exploration of procurement data. The FPDS organizes information into a hierarchical structure, comprising Level 1, Level 2, and Level 3 Categories. This arrangement allows for a progressively granular analysis of the government's purchasing activities. For example, within the realm of "Information and Communication Technology," which could be considered a broader Level 2 Category, a specific Level 3 Category might be "Application”. This specific product categorization helps identify the most relevant contract vehicles for the type of product or service an agency is looking to procure. By leveraging Level 3 Categories, stakeholders can pinpoint the exact nature of services procured and see how many Prime Vendors are selling goods in services in a category.

Obligations trends

The Obligations View, intended for observing general purchasing patterns rather than detailed spend analysis, highlights departmental purchasing trends and fiscal year obligations as recorded in FPDS.

By providing insights into general purchasing patterns across federal departments this section aids stakeholders in discerning purchasing trends in the rapidly evolving sectors of Generative AI and Specialized Computing Infrastructure. Stakeholders can leverage this information to gain insights into how procurement strategies in these areas shift over time for Departments. For example, if you filter for Specialized Computing Infrastructure the Obligations view will tell you which Departments are the biggest purchasers of Specialized Computing Infrastructure and how obligations for Specialized Computing Infrastructure have been trending from FY20 to present.

PSC

The PSC View leverages Product Service Codes (PSC). Each PSC is a four-character code employed by the United States federal government to classify the products, services, and research and development (R&D) items it acquires. These codes are a standardized system designed to categorize and document government procurement transactions efficiently. Utilized across various procurement platforms, these codes are also reported to FPDS.

By utilizing this view, dashboard users can gain insights into the availability of Prime Vendors across PSC Codes. It highlights not just the quantity of Prime Vendors within each PSC code, but also differentiates them based on their business size classification—small or other than small businesses.

NAICS

The NAICS View employs the North American Industry Classification System (NAICS) codes to provide a view of Prime Vendors activities per NAICS code. NAICS codes are structured as a six-digit hierarchical system that categorizes the primary business activities of Prime Vendors. This classification system delineates the economy into progressively specific categories, as represented by the length and composition of each code:

  • The first two digits identify the broad economic sector,
  • The third digit specifies the subsector,
  • The fourth digit defines the industry group,
  • The fifth digit describes the industry itself,
  • The sixth digit is reserved for national industry specifications.

By utilizing this view, dashboard users can gain insights into the availability of Prime Vendors across various economic sectors. It highlights not just the quantity of Prime Vendors within each NAICS code, but also differentiates them based on their size classification. This view helps get a deeper understanding of the Prime Vendors distribution and their principal areas of operation.

According to FAR regulation 19.102(a)(2), the Office of Management and Budget (OMB), through its Economic Classification Policy Committee, updates NAICS codes every five years. The U.S. Census Bureau maintains a searchable database of NAICS codes on its North American Industry Classification System website.

Available contracts

This segment of the dashboard focuses on the Procurement Instrument Identification, commonly referred to as PIID, Reference PIID, or Contract ID. The PIID is an identifier within government procurement, acting as a unique number assigned to each contract, agreement, or order. It serves as a key for conducting in-depth research on specific contract vehicles, especially those pertaining to the acquisition of Generative AI and Specialized Computing Infrastructure.

In this view, users are presented with a list of PIIDs or Contract IDs corresponding to active and past contracts involved in procuring Generative AI and Specialized Computing Infrastructure. This inclusion helps acquisition workforce members and decision-makers seeking to explore and analyze contracts that they may be able to consult for more information. The PIIDs provided in the dashboard are gateways to contractual information. By using these identifiers, users can access detailed contract documents, performance reports, financial terms, and Prime Vendor information that may help inform their own Generative AI and Specialized Computing Infrastructure acquisitions.
