Executive Order on the Safe, Secure, & Trustworthy Development & Use of Artificial Intelligence

On October 30, 2023, the Biden Administration unveiled a groundbreaking Executive Order on the Safe, Secure, and Trustworthy Development and Use of Artificial Intelligence (AI). This pivotal initiative sets forth a strategic framework aimed at promoting the ethical development and application of AI across diverse sectors and government entities. This page provides an in-depth examination of the Executive Order's objectives, guiding principles, and its anticipated impact on the evolution of AI in the United States.

The issuance of this Executive Order marks a significant advancement in establishing the necessary oversight and safeguards, while simultaneously encouraging innovation that has the potential to significantly enhance the services provided by the government. Following the directives of the Executive Order, the Office of Management and Budget has formulated preliminary policy guidelines to encourage the innovative application of AI within Federal agencies, ensuring that such advancements are pursued with a commitment to responsibility.

Intended Takeaways

  • Strengthening AI governance through the designation of Chief AI Officer that hold primary responsibility for managing risk from their agency’s use of AI.
  • Advancing responsible AI innovation through removal of barriers to the responsible use of AI and the development of agency AI strategies.
  • Managing risks from the use of AI, through the adoption of minimum AI risk management practices for uses of AI that impact rights and safety.

About OMB

The Office of Management and Budget (OMB) supports the President of the United States by ensuring the President's vision is executed throughout the Executive Branch. Its mission includes aiding the President in achieving policy, budget, management, and regulatory goals, as well as carrying out the agency's legal duties.

About ITVMO

The Information Technology Vendor Management Office (ITVMO) serves as a trusted advisor and advocate for Federal IT acquisitions. Established in October 2020 within the Governmentwide Category Management Framework by the Office of Management and Budget (OMB), the ITVMO aims to address the challenges faced by agencies in IT vendor relationship management, including standardizing technical requirements, mitigating cyber-risk, improving data quality, and leveraging the government's buying power to drive improvements and business transformation.

The ITVMO's mission is to provide agencies with meaningful intelligence to inform and support faster, smarter IT buying decisions. It operates within the Federal IT Acquisitions Ecosystem, collaborating with various stakeholders, including agency IT buyers, vehicle solution holders, federal and industry partners, and suppliers (and resellers). This collaboration aims to standardize requirements, increase market compliance, and drive smarter, faster IT acquisitions.

The ITVMO offers a coordinated voice representing all agencies to the vendor community, focusing on customer-centric solutions, convenience, cost savings, and compliance.
